Memefly

Deep Learning App that generates Dank Memes

For millenniums, artists and writers have been able to generate captions from images or from images to paintings. It goes as far back to ancient Egyptian tomb paintings from 2000 B.C., to classic Chinese paintings from 1000 A.D. to the popular memes of the 2010s.

For Memefly, we attempted to generate memes from image inputs. We trained a GRU network par-injected with image embedding extracted using pre-trained InceptionV3 and sentence embedding using methods outlined in Show and Tell: A Neural Image Caption Generator [Vinyals â€˜14], Where to put the Image in an Image Caption Generator [Tanti â€˜17], and Dank Learning: Generating Memes Using Deep Neural Networks[Peirson â€˜18]. We also implemented beam-search as suggested by Professor Andrew Ng. We scrapped from various online resources a small dataset of around 108 memes and 20,000 descriptions for our training data. We implemented our network using Tensorflow 2.0, and used offline learning to generate captions and store them in Cloud MongoDB database.
